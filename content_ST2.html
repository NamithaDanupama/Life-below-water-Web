Introduction: Why Marine Conservation Matters?

The ocean is the lifeblood of our planet, covering over 70% of the Earth's surface and producing more than 50% of our oxygen. It regulates climate, supports biodiversity, and provides food and livelihoods for billions. However, human activities, including overfishing, pollution, and climate change, threaten marine ecosystems. Conservation efforts aim to restore balance and ensure a sustainable future for life below water.

1. Marine Protected Areas (MPAs) üåç

What Are MPAs?

Marine Protected Areas are designated sections of the ocean where human activities like fishing, drilling, and tourism are restricted to conserve biodiversity. As of 2023, only 8% of the world's oceans are protected, but the UN aims to expand this to 30% by 2030 under the 30x30 initiative.

Why Are MPAs Important?

  • Help rebuild fish populations by reducing overfishing.
  • Protect critical habitats like coral reefs, seagrass meadows, and mangroves.
  • Serve as natural barriers against coastal erosion and extreme weather.
